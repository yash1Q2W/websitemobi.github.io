Women Empowerment

There is nothing stronger than a broken woman who has rebuilt herself. 

Empowering women does not mean to give special rights to the females. It does not mean to have pity on them. It means to share the equality among both, the males and the females and also all other genders out there. Empowering women means to have the equal rights such as men. It refers to make women powerful to make them capable of deciding for themselves. The society teach girls shame, to close legs, cover yourselves, we make them feel as though being born female they’re already guilty of something. As so, girls grow up to be a women who cannot say they have dreams to fulfil and goals to achieve. They grow up to be a women who silence themselves. They grow up to be a women who cannot say what they truely think. And they grow up to be a women who have turned pretence into an art form.

The world is progressing each and everyday but the world has not seen the same kind of progress when it comes to gender equality. We need women empowerment more than ever. Education is very regressive in India for women. One in three women is a victim of domestic violence and abuse. Women do face a lot of problems here, and thus, empowering women is the need of the hour.Women consistently trail men in formal labour force participation, access to credit, savings rates, income levels, entrepreneurship rates, as well as in inheritance and ownership rights.





Our NGO, Inaayat Foundation, Vadodara has planned to start Gruh Udhyog where women who were a victim of domestic violence or are widows, women who have faced financial instabilities, and basically just helpless women are provided with employment. With the idea of starting Gruh Udhyog these women can become independent, strong, and capable of speaking for themselves. Various training must be held to ensure proper skill development of these women so that they can fend for themselves and thus live an independent and happy life. Some of the products that will be manufactured at Gruh Udhyog are paper bags, paper cups, paper glasses, incense sticks (agarbatti) and food items like aachar, papad, khakhra, etc. All the profits shall be helpful to feed the deprived people.
